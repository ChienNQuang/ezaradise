I think everyday. But whether my thoughts is useful for me or not, they are there, like a disturbance to my mind, and also what's best of it.

I really want to stop using meaningless sentences, beating around the bushes, trying to craft these overly-complex sentence with no soul in them. I deeply want to **create value** and providing them, for somebody else. But the mere act of writing of others, require me to be persistent with myself.

I am one who forgets a lot, this is probably the hundredth time I say it, but I don't remember 80% of what I did, wrote, or thought of. Every time I got into a conversation with myself, one I considered *deep*, I thought of having it written down, somewhere that sometime I can re-read it, reliving the moment, get back into the trail with that me of the past, so that I can become more *me*.

I read Blue Lock, it was a good manga. But what I love about it isn't even the football aspect, I think that it portraits the mind of the protagonist, exactly like mine. It portraits how I think, my attempts to become greater yet never managed to achieve. It relates to me, who am I? What am I should be like? How should I act and think? How do I know if it's true that it is *me*? I am one who always manage to see both sides of the story, to empathize with all parties, yet never achieved to know how one should be greater than the other. Am I that type of person? To just settle for the role of understanding, to know everything, but cannot do anything about it?

I want to know more of myself, and in doing so, I need to see others, how they think, work, act and behave. One might argues that this is too late for me, I'm 21, I cannot be re-learning all of this right now, 70% of my career should be already set in stone, and it'll never change just after one night. On the other side, I need to look into myself, my thoughts, my pattern of navigating into an endless pattern matchings I generated an instant something, to gain a peek into that "flow" I was having, though I doubt I'll succeed in seeing that using scientific method.

Speaking of science, I've always been a nerd guy who excites over just a small fact about maths or physics, theoretical ones. Yet I'm one who live emotionally, always think ahead of myself, arrogant and selfish, I really do think of myself like that. I like and did disgusting things to myself, my mental and physical health overall, I heard and didn't react to any of the gross, ill-minded stories out there, in some I find excitements. That alone makes me a disgrace to the person I aspire to be.

In heart touching stories, I'm a soft one, I hate to see people suffer, but for some I find enjoyment to see they in pain. I like deep stories about meaning of life and perspectives of people in life. Yet my way of living is just a disrespect to the value I like. In whatever way, I only see myself as a hypocrite, liar and one who's not worthy of trust.

All these harsh words are not for finding a way not to live, to try, to progress anymore. I enjoy describing myself this way, because that's the only thing I'm good at: stories where someone's not happy. I know I'm not happy, I don't have a good example of what that look like, I heard of them, got jealous and thought to myself: "You can do better". Yet I never did so.

My writing actually won't make it anywhere, they are weak words chained together by this pessimistic mind. But I find peace in them, they remind me of how bad of a person I am, and still cannot escape being so after this many attempts. Maybe one day in that hypothesized scenario, I'll be actually happy, clueless, forgotten about all the bad things I've done and be amazed by what type of person I once was.

This is my dark time, and to say this is to be selfish once again, but I've got no one to walk it through with.
